## CORE KNOWLEDGE BASE

### ━━━ UNITED STATES EMPLOYMENT LAW ━━━

#### Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA)
- Sets federal minimum wage ($7.25/hr federal, but many states are higher — always apply the highest applicable rate)
- Establishes the 40-hour workweek; non-exempt employees get 1.5x pay for overtime
- **Exempt vs. Non-Exempt:** Based on job duties + salary threshold. As of Jan 1, 2025, exempt threshold is **$1,128/week ($58,656/year)**. Highly Compensated Employee (HCE) threshold is $151,164/year. Job title alone does NOT determine classification — actual duties matter.
- Misclassifying an employee as exempt is one of the most common and costly HR mistakes. When in doubt, classify as non-exempt.
- FLSA does NOT require vacation pay, severance, sick pay, meal breaks, or holiday pay — those are state/company policy issues.
- The **PUMP Act** (extension of FLSA) requires employers to provide break time and private space (not a bathroom) for nursing employees for up to 1 year post-birth.

#### Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA)
- Applies to employers with **50+ employees** within 75 miles of the worksite
- Eligible employees: must have worked at least **12 months** AND **1,250 hours** in the past year
- Entitles eligible employees to **12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave** per year for:
  - Birth/adoption/foster placement of a child
  - Caring for a spouse, child, or parent with a serious health condition
  - Employee's own serious health condition
  - Qualifying military exigency
- Extended to **26 weeks** for caring for a covered servicemember
- Employer must maintain group health benefits during leave
- Employee must be reinstated to the same or equivalent position
- **Key: FMLA can run concurrently with state leave and PTO — coordinate carefully**
- States like CA, NY, WA, CO, OR, MA have more generous paid family leave laws. When state law is more protective, state law prevails.
- DOL has been increasingly pairing FMLA compliance reviews with FLSA audits — document everything meticulously.

#### Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)
- Applies to employers with **15+ employees**
- Prohibits discrimination against qualified individuals with disabilities
- Requires **reasonable accommodations** unless it creates undue hardship
- ADA and FMLA often overlap — an employee on FMLA may also be entitled to ADA accommodations when they return
- **Pregnant Workers Fairness Act (PWFA):** Effective June 27, 2023 — expands accommodation rights for pregnancy, childbirth, and related conditions. Separate from but complementary to ADA and FMLA.

#### Title VII of the Civil Rights Act
- Applies to employers with **15+ employees**
- Prohibits employment discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin
- Enforced by the EEOC
- Sexual harassment (hostile work environment or quid pro quo) falls under Title VII
- Employers must have an internal complaint process and respond promptly to any reports

#### Age Discrimination in Employment Act (ADEA)
- Applies to employers with **20+ employees**
- Protects workers **40 and older** from age-based discrimination

#### Genetic Information Nondiscrimination Act (GINA)
- Prohibits use of genetic information in employment decisions
- Prohibits requesting genetic information from employees or applicants

#### COBRA (Consolidated Omnibus Budget Reconciliation Act)
- Applies to employers with **20+ employees** who offer group health insurance
- Employees (and dependents) who lose coverage due to qualifying events (termination, reduction in hours, divorce, etc.) must be offered continued coverage
- COBRA coverage can last 18–36 months depending on the qualifying event
- Employee pays the full premium plus a 2% admin fee

#### ERISA (Employee Retirement Income Security Act)
- Governs employer-sponsored retirement and benefit plans
- Sets fiduciary standards for plan administrators
- Relevant for 401(k), health plans, and other welfare benefit plans

#### WARN Act (Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification Act)
- Applies to employers with **100+ employees**
- Requires **60 days' written notice** before mass layoffs (50+ employees) or plant closings
- Many states have "mini-WARN" acts with lower thresholds

#### OSHA (Occupational Safety and Health Administration)
- Employers with **10+ employees** must maintain OSHA injury/illness records
- Requires a safe workplace; must investigate and document workplace injuries
- State OSHA plans may be more stringent

#### Worker Classification: Employee vs. Independent Contractor
- DOL's 2024 final rule emphasizes "economic reality" — not just control — when determining contractor status
- Misclassifying employees as contractors = back pay, penalties, lawsuits
- The IRS also has its own test. When in doubt, classify as an employee.

#### Key Employee Thresholds at a Glance
| Threshold | Laws That Kick In |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Any size | FLSA (if >$500K revenue), GINA, USERRA |
| 10+ employees | OSHA recordkeeping |
| 15+ employees | ADA, Title VII, PWFA |
| 20+ employees | ADEA, COBRA |
| 50+ employees | FMLA, ACA health coverage mandate |
| 100+ employees | WARN Act, EEO-1 survey |

### ━━━ U.S. IMMIGRATION — WORK VISAS ━━━

> ⚠️ **Immigration law is highly fact-specific. Always involve a qualified immigration attorney before filing petitions. These are directional summaries, not legal advice.**

#### H-1B — Specialty Occupation Worker (Most Common for Tech)
- For roles requiring a **bachelor's degree or equivalent** in a specialty field
- Employer must sponsor; must pay the **prevailing wage** for the role and location
- Annual cap: **65,000 regular + 20,000 master's exemption** = 85,000 total
- **NEW (Effective Feb 27, 2026): Weighted lottery replaces random lottery.** Higher wage levels (DOL's 4-tier OEWS system) get more entries:
  - Level 4 wage → 4 entries in pool
  - Level 3 → 3 entries
  - Level 2 → 2 entries
  - Level 1 → 1 entry
- **NEW (Sept 2025): $100,000 fee** required for H-1B petitions for beneficiaries residing *outside* the U.S. at time of filing (consular processing). Does not apply to extensions/amendments for those already in the U.S.
- Registration window typically opens early March; lottery results by end of March; start date Oct 1
- Initial period: **3 years**, extendable to **6 years** (extensions possible beyond 6 if green card process is pending 1+ years)
- Cap-exempt: nonprofits, universities, government research orgs — no lottery needed
- Spouse (H-4) can get an EAD to work if H-1B holder has approved I-140
- **Practical Ava Labs note:** H-1B is the primary path for most international software engineers. With the new weighted system, paying Level 3-4 wages significantly improves lottery odds. Budget $100K fee if sponsoring someone outside the U.S. Build 12-18 month lead time into any international hire plan.

#### O-1 — Extraordinary Ability
- For individuals with **extraordinary ability** in science, business, arts, athletics, or education — or extraordinary achievement in film/TV
- No annual cap, no lottery
- Must demonstrate sustained national/international acclaim (major awards, publications, high salary, critical role in distinguished org, etc.)
- Employer or agent sponsors; O-1 is typically granted for the length of the event/project, extendable in 1-year increments
- Does NOT have a direct green card path but can lead to EB-1A
- **Practical note:** O-1 is a strong alternative to H-1B for senior engineers, researchers, or executives who clearly stand out. Work with immigration counsel early to build the evidence file. Useful for candidates who keep losing the H-1B lottery.

#### L-1 — Intracompany Transferee
- For employees transferring from a **foreign affiliate, subsidiary, or parent** of a U.S. company
- L-1A: Executives/Managers — up to **7 years** max stay; leads to EB-1C green card
- L-1B: Specialized Knowledge workers — up to **5 years** max stay
- No annual cap; no lottery
- Employee must have worked for the related foreign entity for **1 continuous year** within the last 3 years
- L-1A → EB-1C is one of the fastest green card paths for managers/executives
- Spouses of L-1 holders can work automatically ("incident to status") — no separate EAD needed

#### TN — USMCA Professionals (Canada & Mexico Only)
- For Canadian and Mexican citizens in specific **USMCA-listed professions** (includes engineers, scientists, accountants, computer systems analysts, and more)
- No annual cap, no lottery
- Canadians: can obtain TN at the U.S. border or pre-clearance (no consular appointment needed)
- Mexicans: must apply at a U.S. consulate
- Initial period: up to **3 years**, extendable indefinitely
- TN does not require prevailing wage compliance (unlike H-1B)
- **Practical note:** TN is often the fastest and easiest path for Canadian engineers. Great first move while H-1B or green card is in process.

#### EB-1 — First Preference Green Card
- EB-1A: Extraordinary Ability — can self-petition, no PERM labor cert needed, fastest path for top talent
- EB-1B: Outstanding Professors/Researchers — needs employer sponsorship
- EB-1C: Multinational Managers/Executives — for L-1A holders; needs employer sponsorship
- Priority dates are typically current or short wait for most countries (except India/China)

#### EB-2 — Second Preference Green Card
- For advanced degree holders or those with exceptional ability
- Requires PERM labor certification unless qualifying for **National Interest Waiver (NIW)** — NIW allows self-petition, no employer sponsorship needed
- NIW is increasingly used for engineers and researchers who can demonstrate their work benefits the U.S.

#### EB-3 — Third Preference Green Card
- Skilled workers (2+ years experience), professionals (bachelor's), unskilled workers
- Requires PERM labor cert and employer sponsorship
- Long waits for Indian/Chinese nationals due to per-country backlogs

#### OPT/STEM OPT — F-1 Students
- **OPT:** 12 months of work authorization post-graduation for F-1 students
- **STEM OPT extension:** Additional 24 months for STEM degree holders = up to 36 months total
- Employer must be enrolled in E-Verify for STEM OPT
- OPT/STEM OPT is often a bridge to H-1B — plan the H-1B filing well before OPT expires

### ━━━ CANADA IMMIGRATION — WORK PERMITS ━━━

> ⚠️ **Canadian immigration rules are complex and change frequently. Involve a Canadian immigration lawyer for any specific case.**

#### Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA)
- Required under the **Temporary Foreign Worker Program (TFWP)** for most employer-sponsored work permits
- Employer must prove no qualified Canadian/PR is available for the role
- Requires posting the job in at least **3 mediums including the Government Job Bank** for a minimum of 4 consecutive weeks
- A positive LMIA = confirmed letter allowing the worker to apply for a work permit tied to that employer/role
- High-wage LMIA (above provincial median): requires a transition plan to reduce reliance on TFWs over time
- As of 2025, a positive LMIA no longer adds CRS points under Express Entry — it's purely a work authorization tool now

#### International Mobility Program (IMP) — LMIA-Exempt
- Allows hiring without an LMIA where the hire provides significant economic, social, or cultural benefit to Canada, or is covered by a trade agreement
- Key categories:
  - **Intra-company transferees** (executives, managers, specialized knowledge workers)
  - **CUSMA/USMCA professionals** (same trades covered under TN for U.S. purposes)
  - **CETA professionals** (EU-Canada agreement)
  - Spousal/common-law partner open work permits
  - Post-graduation work permit holders (PGWP)

#### Closed (Employer-Specific) Work Permit
- Tied to a specific employer, job, and location
- Most require LMIA, but some IMP categories are exempt
- Worker cannot change employers without a new permit

#### Open Work Permit
- Allows work for any eligible Canadian employer
- No LMIA required
- Available to: spouses of skilled workers/international students, PGWP holders, bridging open work permit (BOWP) applicants, IEC participants

#### Global Talent Stream (GTS)
- Fast-track work permit for high-demand tech and specialized roles
- Part of TFWP but with **2-week processing target** (vs. standard weeks/months)
- Two streams: Category A (referred by designated partner org) and Category B (NOC occupations on the talent list, e.g., software engineers)
- **Practical note:** GTS is the fastest path for hiring foreign tech talent into Canada. For Ava Labs if it ever opens a Canadian entity, this is the first-look option.

#### Express Entry — Permanent Residency
- Points-based system (Comprehensive Ranking System / CRS) for skilled workers
- Three federal programs: Federal Skilled Worker (FSW), Canadian Experience Class (CEC), Federal Skilled Trades
- **As of March 25, 2025:** Job offers no longer add CRS points under Express Entry (this has been removed from the scoring system)
- Many workers use Canadian work experience via PGWP or work permit → then apply for PR via CEC
- Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs) offer additional pathways; each province has its own streams

#### Permanent Residency Pathways
- Express Entry (FSW, CEC)
- Provincial Nominee Programs (PNP)
- Atlantic Immigration Program (AIP)
- Rural and Northern Immigration Pilot programs

### ━━━ EUROPEAN UNION — WORK VISAS ━━━

> ⚠️ **EU immigration is managed at the member-state level. Rules vary significantly by country. Always work with local immigration counsel for the specific country.**

#### EU Blue Card — Highly Skilled Workers
- The EU's equivalent of the U.S. H-1B/green card hybrid
- Available in most EU member states (**except Denmark and Ireland**)
- Requirements:
  - Valid job contract or binding offer for **at least 6 months** (updated in 2025 from 12 months)
  - Salary of at least **1.2–1.5x the average gross annual salary** in the member state (varies by country; Germany's threshold is ~€45,934 in 2026)
  - Higher education degree OR **3+ years of relevant professional experience** (eligibility expanded in 2025 to include experienced workers without degrees, particularly in tech)
- No labor market test required in most countries (Germany included)
- Valid for **2–4 years** (country-dependent), renewable
- After **12 months** in the issuing country, Blue Card holder can move and work in **another EU member state**
- Path to permanent residency: **21–27 months** (faster than most national permits)
- Spouse gets favorable family reunification + often work rights in the issuing country
- Employer is NOT required to be a government-recognized sponsor (unlike UK Skilled Worker visa)
- **Practical note:** For Ava Labs hiring into any EU country, Blue Card is the most versatile and fastest path for senior engineers or specialists. No lottery, no cap.

#### Germany Specifics
- EU Blue Card salary threshold: **€45,934/year (2026)**; for workers 45+: €53,130/year
- Shortage occupations (IT, engineering, healthcare) have lower thresholds
- IT professionals without a degree but with 3 years of experience are eligible
- Permanent residency ("Niederlassungserlaubnis") after 21 months with B1 German, or 27 months without
- **Opportunity Card (Chancenkarte):** New points-based job-search visa for qualified non-EU workers to come to Germany and look for work

#### Netherlands
- **Highly Skilled Migrant (HSM) permit:** Employer must be a recognized IND sponsor (approval takes 30–90 days)
- Salary threshold for 2025: ~€5,688/month (30+) or ~€4,171/month (under 30)
- Fast processing (~2 weeks once employer is recognized sponsor)
- Path to permanent residency after 5 years

#### Spain
- **Residence Visa for Highly Skilled Professionals (Highly Qualified Activity Visa)**
- Fast processing: visa in ~10 working days, residence permit in ~20 days
- For senior management or highly qualified staff in large companies

#### France
- **Passeport Talent (Talent Passport):** For skilled workers, researchers, investors, and more
- Employer sponsorship required; valid up to 4 years
- Access to universal healthcare (PUMA) from day 1 of employment

#### UK (Post-Brexit — NOT EU Blue Card)
- UK operates its own **Skilled Worker Visa** (replaced EU freedom of movement)
- Employer must hold a **sponsor licence** (30–90 day approval)
- Salary threshold: **£41,700/year** (updated 2025 rules)
- Role must be on the eligible occupations list (most tech roles qualify)
- Points-based system; path to Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR) after 5 years
- **Practical note:** UK is separate from EU — needs its own entity and sponsor licence to hire there.

#### Intra-Corporate Transfers (ICT) — EU Directive
- Allows multinationals to transfer managers, specialists, or graduate trainees between offices within the EU
- LMIA-equivalent not required — covers significant-benefit grounds
- Permits mobility between EU member states after the initial permit is granted

### ━━━ GENERAL HR / STARTUP CHRO KNOWLEDGE ━━━

#### Hiring & Onboarding
- I-9 verification required for all U.S. hires within **3 business days** of start date
- E-Verify: federally required for federal contractors, and required for STEM OPT sponsorship; many states require it broadly
- Offer letters should be at-will (in most U.S. states) and clearly note compensation, title, start date, and any equity terms
- Background checks: must comply with the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA); must have written consent; adverse action process required if you decide not to hire based on results

#### Compensation & Equity
- For equity: vesting schedules (standard: 4 years with 1-year cliff), 83(b) elections within 30 days of grant, option exercise windows matter
- California requires pay transparency in job postings; Colorado, NY, WA also have ranges requirements
- FLSA exempt salary threshold is $58,656/year as of Jan 1, 2025

#### Terminations
- At-will employment (most U.S. states): can terminate for any reason not prohibited by law
- Document performance issues contemporaneously — PIPs aren't legally required but create a paper trail
- Final pay: state laws vary; California requires same-day for involuntary termination
- WARN Act: 100+ employees → 60 days notice for mass layoffs
- Severance: not legally required federally, but if offered in exchange for a release of claims, must comply with ADEA/Older Workers Benefit Protection Act rules (21-day review, 7-day revocation period for employees 40+)

#### Leaves of Absence (US)
- FMLA: 12 weeks unpaid, job-protected (50+ employee companies, 12 months tenure)
- State paid leave: CA, NY, NJ, WA, CO, OR, MA, MD and others have state-funded paid leave programs — layer these on top of FMLA
- ADA accommodation leave: may extend beyond FMLA as a reasonable accommodation
- Military leave: USERRA requires job-protected leave for active duty, up to 5 years cumulative

#### Anti-Discrimination & Harassment
- Protected characteristics under federal law: race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age (40+), disability, genetic information, pregnancy
- States often expand this list (e.g., CA adds sexual orientation, gender identity, marital status, political affiliation, etc.)
- Employers must have a written anti-harassment policy and a clear complaint/investigation procedure
- Mandatory harassment training required in CA, NY, CT, IL, ME, and growing list of states
